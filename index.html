Biography

  • November 6, 1926The tenth child in the family , Hilary Hinton "Zig" Ziglar was born in Coffee Country in Alabama.
  • 1931 When he was 5 yeast old, his father got a job in Mississippi He grew up in Yazoo City in Mississippi where his family moved when Hilary was 5 years old.
  • 1932 Zig Zigler's was just 6 when he lost his father and little sister.
  • 1943 When he was 16, he enrolled in the Navy V-12 Navy College Training Program at the University of South Carolina in Columbia.
  • 1946 Zig Ziglar married Jean. They had four children: Suzan, Tom, Cindy, and Julie.
  • 1947 Zig Ziglar dropped out of college and found a job selling cooking equipment with WearEver Cookware company in Lancaster.
  • 1950 Zig Ziggler was promoted as sales supervisor.
  • 1963 He became a charter member in the establishment of American Salesmasters.
  • 1965 Ziglar became a major sales trainer for Mary Kay Cosmetics while continue speak extensively for audiences of the National Association of Sales Education (NASE).
  • 1968He became a vice president and training director for the Automotive Performance company and moved to Dallas, Texas.
  • 1970 Ziglar founded the Zigmanship Institute, later known as Ziglar, Inc., after this. Subsequently, Ziglar spoke extensively at seminars for motivational speaker Peter Lowe and eventually signed an exclusive agreement to support Peter Lowe events.
  • 1974 First book “See you at the top” was published. It is classic motivational and self-improvement book that has sold more than 1.6 million copies in hardcover.
  • 1978 First autobiography written by Zig Ziglar, his testimony of the man serving the Lord full of joy and excitement.
  • 1984 Zig offered quintessence of his wisdom to all sort of people who need to persuade others in ethical successful way in his bestseller "Secrets of Closing the Sale."
  • 2001 Zig Ziglar achieved the Silver Buffalo Award from Boy Scouts of America which is the highest award in the United States for services to the youth.
  • 2009 Zig Ziglar Servant Leadership Award was established. The purpose of this reward is to encapsulate the legacy of Zig Ziglar by annually naming a recipient who embodies the very essence of his servant leadership.
  • November 28, 2012 Ziglar died from pneumonia at a hospital in Plano, Texas, leaving estate worth 15 million dollars. He wrote more than two dozen books with total sales well into the millions. His books and audios have been translated into over thirty-eight languages and dialects. For forty years he delivered powerful messages to help millions of people reach their goals.

Zig Ziglar's 9 rules for success

  1. Have a dream and set goals

    A goal properly set is halfway reached.

  2. Think as a champion

    Your attitude, not your aptitude, will determine your altitude.

  3. Plan

    In order to win in life, you must plan to win, prepare to win, and then and only then can you expect to win.

  4. Do it now

    You do not need to be great to start but you have to start to be great.

  5. Defy the odds

    Happiness is not pleasure – happiness is victory! Remember that failure is an event, not a person.

  6. Evaluate where you are

    • Are You Happy?
    • Are You Healthy?
    • Are You Reasonably Prosperous?
    • Are You Secure?
    • Do You Have Friends?
    • Do You Have Peace of Mind?
    • Do You Have Good Family Relationships?
    • Do You Have Hope?

  7. Learn how to sell

    Why America is great? We are the land of salespeople!

  8. Prime the pump

    Say no to the tears of the moment to say yes to the laughter of a lifetime. Regardless of what you are doing, if you pump long enough, hard enough, and enthusiastically enough, sooner or later the effort will bring forth.

  9. Provide value

    If you go looking for a friend, you are going to find they’re scarce. If you go out to be a friend, you will find them everywhere.
